The Federal Communications Commission's decision to broaden its covered equipment list to include foreign-manufactured consumer routers marks a significant escalation in regulatory oversight of residential networking infrastructure. Previously focused on network-adjacent hardware, the expansion brings consumer Wi-Fi equipment under the agency's security microscope—a move that simultaneously addresses legitimate security concerns and reflects broader anxieties about foreign technology supply chains.

The 220 comments accompanying this announcement reveal sharp division in how stakeholders perceive the action.
